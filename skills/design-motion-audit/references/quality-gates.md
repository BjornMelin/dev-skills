# Quality Gates

## Creative quality

- The motion concept is clear in one sentence.
- The screen has one primary hero idea.
- Supporting motion is quieter than the hero moment.
- Motion demonstrates product value, not decoration.
- The same branded motif appears across web and native where appropriate.

## Implementation quality

- Motion constants are tokenized.
- R3F hot paths mutate refs and use delta time.
- Reanimated hot paths use shared values and worklets.
- Gesture release uses velocity.
- Animations are interruptible.
- Reduced motion exists and preserves clarity.
- Performance budget is explicit.

## Visual quality

- Depth hierarchy is readable.
- Objects are grounded with shadow or intentionally floating.
- Materials match their physical behavior.
- Lights reveal form.
- Postprocessing is restrained.
- Text remains legible.

## Final response checklist

Every implementation response should include:

1. Files changed.
2. New or updated motion tokens.
3. R3F or Reanimated technique used.
4. Performance safeguards.
5. Reduced-motion behavior.
6. Manual verification steps.
7. Known risks.
